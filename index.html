We came up with the mission of accelerating the development of interplanetary transport, first to Mars, then to other planets in our solar system, and in the future, places outside of it.


Before the creation of SpaceX, mainly in the late 1980s, NASA launched its astronauts into space via space shuttle. The problem with this was the great cost involved. To make space travel cheaper and more frequent, on March 14 2002, Elon Musk founded SpaceX. To revolutionize space technology.


The first rocket built by SpaceX, developed from 2006 to 2009, the Falcon 1, revolutionized the sector by being much cheaper to produce and to operate. At that time, the sector was dominated by spaceships built by public institutions and/or government-funded companies such as Boening and Lockheed Martin.


Today we have it in full operation, having already carried out more than 180 launches and taken almost 4000 tons of mass to low earth orbit, falcon 9, our most used service. Next to that we have the Falcon Heavy, also fully functional, and scheduled for three more launches this year.
